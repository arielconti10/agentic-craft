## Staleness risks

- **The document's freshest claims are its riskiest.** Everything dated March–June 2026 (Dynamic Workflows, Gemini Spark, GPT-5.5/Canvas retirement, Opus 4.7/4.8, "Mythos"/"Fable" naming, Conductor, Devin 2.2, Perplexity Model Council) sits in a window where a single vendor changelog can invalidate it. §10's model-version inventory will be stale within a quarter regardless of accuracy.
- **Secondary-source load-bearing walls.** Canvas retirement (krasa.ai), ChatGPT Dreaming (mindstudio.ai), Perplexity 95% recall (supermemory.ai — a competitor), ultracode (developersdigest.tech), CSA taxonomy (zylos.ai, with an internal date contradiction). These are exactly the domains that vanish or were never authoritative.
- **The canonical-model citation is missing.** The "Knight First Amendment Institute / Anthropic Feb 2026" 5-level framing has no URL anywhere in the doc, including Appendix B. If the guide goes public, this is the first thing a skeptical reader will pull on.
- **"Ultracode" vs "ultrathink."** Claude Code's established keyword is "ultrathink"; if "ultracode" never shipped, §9.2's trigger table and its `Option+W` / violet-highlight detail are confabulation dressed in specificity.
- **Terminology drift already visible:** Gemini "Instructions for Gemini" vs the shipped "Saved info"; "Perplexity Computer" appearing only once, unsourced; "5×5" mischaracterization of the DeepMind paper (a permanent error, not a staleness one).
- **Fast-decaying negatives:** every "no product has shipped X" claim (per-topic memory, cost surfaces, renegotiation, root-cause attribution) was at best true at write time; each doubles as the sales pitch for an agentic-craft component, so a rotted negative claim damages the registry's justification, not just the prose.
- **Internal drift between doc and code:** the research doc canonizes L1–L5 (Operator→Observer) while the shipped autonomy-contract pattern page uses suggest/recommend/execute-confirm/execute/initiate. Whichever is newer, the other is stale — and publishing both invites the "which is your actual model?" question.

## Quality read

This is distinctly better than pattern-catalog filler: it takes real positions (L2-by-default against shipping counter-evidence, ledger-not-magic memory, anti-sludge risk-tiering), tags its own confidence, flags its own secondary sources, and — unusually — its registry mostly keeps the receipts, with the heaviest prose bets (Dynamic Workflows, memory ledger, effective policy) backed by the heaviest components (a 1,611-line workflow monitor, a real ledger row, a real policy preview).
